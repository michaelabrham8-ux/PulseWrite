PulseWrite (LinkedIn Ghostwriter) is a Chrome extension that does one main thing very well:
It sits inside LinkedIn and helps you write posts faster and better by using Groq to generate content that sounds like you.
What it actually does (step-by-step user experience)

You open LinkedIn and click the compose post box (the normal way you always start writing).
A small floating button or icon appears near the text area (from the extension).
You click it → a tiny panel opens with two main choices:
“Generate Ideas” → analyzes your last 10–20 posts (or profile if you have few) → gives you 5 ready-to-use post ideas in your style.
Example output:
“Share a quick lesson from your latest client win (motivational tone)”
“Post a contrarian take on AI job loss (thought leadership)”
“Celebrate a small win with humor”

“Write Post” → you type a topic or rough draft → AI writes a full post (or thread) in your voice.
You can choose tone (professional, casual, savage, inspirational) and length (short post, medium, 3-part thread).

Once generated, you see:
The full text
Estimated read time & character count
One-click “Insert into LinkedIn” button → pastes it directly into the compose box

You can edit, post, or regenerate if it’s off.

That’s literally it.
Why users pay for this
Most people hate writing LinkedIn posts but know they need to post 3–5 times/week to grow visibility.
They already pay $10–30/mo for Jasper/Copy.ai but those tools feel generic.
PulseWrite feels native because:

It learns your tone from your own posts
It inserts directly (no copy-paste hell)
It’s cheaper ($9.99/mo) and built specifically for LinkedIn

Free vs Pro limits (to avoid abuse)

Free: 10 generations/month (enough to try)
Pro: unlimited generations + priority Groq model + save favorite tones/templates

No scraping, no APIs that cost money to run, no daily limits that frustrate users.
That’s the product in plain English: a smart typewriter that lives inside LinkedIn and writes posts in your voice.
